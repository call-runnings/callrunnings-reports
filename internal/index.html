Rep Performance Observations

**Where They Struggled:**

Call control and value proposition delivery were consistently weak. Most calls ended passively without attempting to create interest or overcome objections. When prospects said they had existing solutions, reps immediately accepted defeat rather than exploring pain points or differentiation.

The transcript with Alex at Fervor shows poor engagement: 'He was reluctant to engage and indicated he might hang up. However, he stayed on the line for a brief moment.' This suggests the rep failed to establish credibility or value quickly enough.

Timing and permission-based calling was problematic. Multiple calls caught people at terrible times (hospital appointments, driving, in meetings, on holiday) yet reps still attempted to have substantive conversations rather than immediately offering to reschedule.

Reps failed to ask qualifying questions. When prospects said they had solutions in place, there was no exploration of contract end dates, satisfaction levels, specific pain points, or gaps in current coverage. The call with Pete Bartley mentioned he was 'overwhelmed by the number of carbon platforms available' but the rep didn't position Further as a way to cut through that confusion.

Objection Handling Analysis

**Common Objections Encountered:**

1. 'We already have a solution' (appeared in ~25 transcripts)
2. 'Not the right person' (appeared in ~15 transcripts)
3. 'No budget' (Kate, Molly)
4. 'Bad timing/too busy' (Sophie, Rebecca at Connect+)
5. 'Recently invested in new tool' (Nick, Natalie)

**How Objections Were Handled:**

Poorly, in almost all cases. Reps treated objections as rejection rather than conversation starters. Standard pattern: Prospect states objection → Rep says 'okay, thanks for your time' → Call ends.

Example of missed opportunity with Amy: 'She stated they already have a different partner for this service and are happy with it, so they're not looking to evaluate options at this time.' The rep could have asked: What platform are they using? What do they like about it? What's challenging? When does the contract renew? Instead, call ended immediately.

Kate Martin expressed interest in managing carbon reporting in-house but struggles to get business sign-off. This is a clear pain point the rep should have explored: What's the barrier to sign-off? What would make the business case compelling? Have they tried building an ROI analysis? Instead, the rep just agreed to 'email information and follow up at end of year.'

Rebecca at Connect+ mentioned 'it's not easy' to capture scope three data despite having systems in place. This admission of pain was completely ignored. The rep should have immediately said, 'That's exactly the challenge we solve - can I show you how we've helped similar companies make scope three collection 10x easier?'

**What Could Be Improved:**

Implement a 3-question rule: Before accepting any objection, ask at least 3 follow-up questions to understand the real situation. Train reps on 'happy with current provider' responses: 'That's great to hear - what platform are you using? How long have you been with them? What made you choose them initially?' This keeps the conversation going and often reveals cracks.

For budget objections, shift to ROI framing: 'I understand - most of our clients felt the same way until they realized manual processes were costing them 20+ hours per month. Would it be worth 15 minutes to see if we could save you that time?'

**Weak Pitching:**

There's almost no evidence of actual pitching in these transcripts. Reps appear to be introducing themselves and immediately being shut down. The value proposition of Further is never clearly articulated. What makes Further different from the 20+ other carbon platforms? Why should someone switch? These calls read like reps are apologizing for calling rather than confidently offering value.

Coaching Recommendations

**Immediate Action Items:**

1. **Implement the 'Three Questions Rule':** Before accepting any objection as final, reps must ask minimum 3 follow-up questions. Create a cheat sheet for each common objection with question prompts. Example for 'We have a solution': (1) What platform are you using? (2) How long have you been with them? (3) What's working well, and what's been challenging?

2. **Revise the Opening Hook:** Current approach is getting immediate shutdowns. Test a pattern interrupt: 'Hi [Name], I know you weren't expecting my call - I've got about 30 seconds to explain why I'm reaching out. Fair enough?' This acknowledges the cold call and gives them control, increasing engagement.

3. **Pain Development Training:** Role-play sessions focused on digging into pain points. When Matthew says scope three is 'time-consuming,' the response should be: 'Time-consuming is exactly what we hear - help me understand, are we talking 5 hours a month or more like 20-30?' Quantify pain before pitching solutions.

4. **Calendar Booking for Callbacks:** Every 'call me back later' should convert to a specific day and time, not vague 'later this week' or 'in a few months.' Example from Gregor transcript: 'He asked me to follow up later this week' - should have said, 'How's Thursday at 2 PM or Friday at 10 AM?'

5. **Contract Intelligence Questions:** When prospects mention existing solutions, train reps to ask: 'When does your contract come up for renewal?' This single question can transform a dead call into a future opportunity with a specific timeline.

6. **Referral Training:** Even failed calls can generate referrals. When someone says 'I'm not the right person,' the response should be: 'I appreciate that - who in your organization handles sustainability reporting?' Several transcripts show this was done (Scott, Jonathan, Samantha) but it's inconsistent.

7. **ROI/Time-Saving Framework:** Develop a simple calculator: 'Most sustainability managers spend 15-20 hours monthly on scope three reporting. If you're spending even half that, and earning £40/hour, that's £4,800 annually in time cost alone. Our platform costs less than that and saves the time.' Make this repeatable.

8. **Email Follow-up Templates:** Multiple prospects agreed to receive emails (Nick, John, Trevor, Elizabeth) but there's no indication of what those emails contain. Create template library with case studies, ROI calculators, and comparison guides that reps can customize.

9. **Objection Reversal Practice:** 'We're happy with our current provider' should become: 'That's great - we love working with educated buyers. What we find is that companies often don't realize they're overpaying or missing features until they see alternatives. Would you be open to a 10-minute comparison call, just so you know you're getting the best deal?'

10. **Meeting Confirmation Process:** For the 4 meetings that were booked, implement 48-hour and 24-hour confirmation sequences with prep materials. Matthew's meeting on May 20th should include a prep email asking him to estimate hours spent monthly on scope three so the demo can be customized.

3. **Wrong Job Titles/Roles:** Several contacts had incorrect role information:
- Matt 'does not handle sustainability reporting'
- Richard Harrison works for 'financial advisors,' not sustainability
- Wrong sustainability manager called entirely
- Karen's role is 'research and learning regarding regulations rather than ESG reporting'
- Samantha handles social value in ESG for Milton Keynes but not at group level

4. **Out-of-Office/Leave Contacts:** Multiple people on holiday or leave (Juliana, Kirsten, Rodden, Claire, sick leave prospect) suggesting data doesn't include OOO information or timing is poor.

5. **Wrong Contact Person:** At least 10 calls reached people who said 'I'm not the right person' but data suggested they were. This indicates LinkedIn scraping or database enrichment is pulling inaccurate titles.

**Patterns Identified:**

- Personal mobile numbers are over-represented in the database (massive compliance risk)
- No system to flag when someone changes companies or roles
- LinkedIn titles may be outdated or misinterpreted
- No suppression list being maintained for people who request removal

**Recommendations:**

1. **Immediate Audit:** Review data source for personal vs. work numbers. Remove all personal mobile numbers unless explicit consent was obtained.

2. **Quarterly Data Refresh:** Implement system to check if contacts are still at companies before campaigns launch. Even basic LinkedIn checks would catch people who've moved.

3. **Suppression List Compliance:** Eleanor, Sophie, Martin, and others requested removal. Ensure these are immediately added to do-not-call list across all campaigns.

4. **Role Verification Step:** Before calling sustainability managers, verify they actually handle carbon reporting/scope three. Job title alone isn't sufficient.

5. **Data Source Review:** If purchasing data, the quality is substandard. Wrong numbers, outdated contacts, and incorrect roles appear in ~20% of calls. This needs immediate vendor review or switch to different data provider.

6. **CRM Updates from Calls:** When contacts provide referrals (Paloma Hemosa, Olivia Fernandez, Liz McGonagall, Sam Davies), these should immediately update CRM with new contact records to replace outdated ones.
