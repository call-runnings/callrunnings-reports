What happened this period
(The honest version)

Nine meetings booked across 3,126 dials — the strongest result of any campaign in the portfolio this period. The Scope 3 pitch is landing. Most prospects know exactly what you're talking about and broadly agree it's a problem.

The main friction isn't the message. It's timing and incumbency. Most larger organisations already have a process or a partner in place, even if that process is held together with spreadsheets and goodwill. Mid-market companies without a dedicated sustainability headcount are the clearest opportunity right now.

A number of conversations ended with "call me back in June or July" — not losses, but pipeline. Worth tracking deliberately.

Asked by prospects who are genuinely evaluating — not a brushoff. They want to understand the product format before committing to a longer conversation.

"What sort of embedded carbon? What materials?"
~2 conversations — construction sector

Construction contacts engage more technically. They want to know whether you're talking about embodied carbon in materials, supply chain Scope 3, or both. A more specific answer for this vertical would help.

"How did you get my number?"
~5 conversations

A recurring concern among sustainability contacts specifically. Data sourcing transparency matters more in this sector than in most.

What the market is telling us
(Read between the lines of 519 conversations)
Scope 3 is universally validated — but most large targets already have something in place

Nobody disputes the problem. The challenge is that for larger organisations, an existing process is almost always running. The real opportunity is mid-market companies that haven't yet formalised their approach, particularly those without a dedicated sustainability headcount.

Sustainability redundancies are creating churn in the contact pool

Several contacts across the period had recently been made redundant or were in transition. Some organisations appear to be quietly deprioritising sustainability headcount. This affects list quality and means existing contacts need re-qualifying more frequently than in previous periods.

Construction and NHS contacts are a different conversation

Construction contacts engage technically and ask about embodied carbon in materials. NHS contacts are constrained by procurement frameworks and group-level authority. Both sub-markets are worth a tailored pitch angle rather than the general Scope 3 opener.

"We're actually doing a base carbon analysis of building an inventory at the moment. And Scope 3 is definitely something we struggle with."
The "call me back in June/July" pipeline is real

Multiple contacts across the period gave a specific future window rather than a flat no. These aren't polite brushoffs — they named months. Track these deliberately as warm pipeline rather than closed conversations.
