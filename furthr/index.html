Campaign Overview

During April 2026, Call Runnings executed 1,518 calls on behalf of Furthr, achieving 134 conversations and securing 9 meetings. This represents a conversation rate of 8.8% and a meeting conversion rate of 6.7% from conversations. The campaign targeted sustainability managers and ESG professionals to discuss carbon reporting and scope three emissions solutions. Of the conversations held, 24 prospects were identified as 'Activated' (demonstrating strong interest or fit), whilst 59 indicated they were definitively not interested. The primary challenge was reach, with 1,287 calls resulting in no answer. The campaign successfully identified decision-makers across various sectors including NHS trusts, housing associations, galleries, universities, and commercial organisations.

Competitor & Market Intelligence

The market is notably crowded with established providers. Watershed emerged as a recognised platform, with one prospect stating they "use a platform called Watershed for carbon reporting and aren't looking to change." PlanetMark appeared multiple times, with prospects reporting recent partnerships: "They've partnered with PlanetMark for their carbon footprint verification" and another noting "they're currently using PlanetMark and managing it themselves." Trace software was mentioned as an existing solution during a company merger scenario. Netscope was cited by one prospect who "is currently using Netscope for tracking their carbon emissions" having just started in January. Gallery-specific solutions were also noted, with one prospect explaining they "use the Gallery Climate Coalition and Judy Bicycle Alban Calculators for carbon logging, which are free and work well for them." The sentiment towards existing providers was generally positive, with prospects expressing satisfaction and stability. Several mentioned consultancy arrangements, with one noting an "ongoing contract until April 1, 2027" and another describing their solution as "straightforward and cost-effective." The data gathering process was notably not seen as problematic by some: one prospect "didn't find data gathering too tricky" with their current platform. The competitive landscape is further complicated by prospects feeling overwhelmed, with one stating they are "satisfied with their current processes and overwhelmed by the number of carbon platforms available."

Common Objections

The dominant objection category was existing provider satisfaction and contractual commitments. Multiple prospects cited long-term contracts: "they have a solution in place that is contractually set for three years," "ongoing contract until April 1, 2027," and one who "recently entered a procurement phase, indicating they've already made a decision." This reveals a market with typical 1-3 year contract cycles. Timing objections were prevalent, with prospects requesting follow-up at specific intervals: "suggested reaching out in six months," "reach out again in July or August," "follow up in a couple of months once their financial year has calmed down," and "follow up next year." Budget constraints appeared as a significant barrier, particularly for smaller organisations. One prospect stated explicitly: "She has no budget for software and is currently using an external consultant for annual reporting. Expressed interest in managing it in-house but struggles to get business sign-off." Another mentioned "time and budget constraints" whilst finding the offering relevant. Wrong contact objections revealed organisational complexity, with carbon reporting often centralised: "carbon reporting is managed at the group level in Germany and not under his responsibility," and another noting "Each country operates independently, and they have their own tools for data submission." Several prospects indicated they were no longer in relevant roles or had left companies, suggesting high turnover in sustainability positions. The internal capability objection emerged from organisations confident in their current approach: "they are managing these internally and feel confident in their progress" and "they have a well-established sustainability team and are meeting their targets without any current challenges."

Prospect Questions & Information Requests

Whilst explicit questions were limited in these transcripts, prospects' information-gathering behaviour revealed their priorities. Several prospects requested email follow-ups with information they could review or share internally: "Suggested I could send information for her to circulate later," "agreed to receive an email with information that he can forward to his colleague," and "requested one email with information, emphasising not to spam him." This indicates a preference for considered evaluation rather than immediate decisions. Prospects demonstrated interest in understanding differentiation and capability, with one agreeing to "check out our website" and another expressing that they would "keep us in mind for the future." The referral pattern showed prospects attempting to direct calls appropriately: "He referred me to Paloma Hemosa, who handles the reporting annually" and "suggested contacting someone at the group level for ESG reporting." One prospect's curiosity about data sourcing emerged: "He was curious about their source" regarding how their contact information was obtained, suggesting sensitivity around data privacy. The methodology gap became apparent with one NHS contact who "isn't currently measuring scope 3 emissions due to unclear methodology," indicating that some organisations recognise gaps but lack clarity on solutions. Prospects also revealed interest in efficiency improvements, particularly around scope three: one "finds time-consuming" the current spreadsheet approach to "scope three reporting" and expressed interest in "more efficient solutions."

Market Themes

Four dominant themes emerged from the conversations. First, market maturity and saturation: the carbon reporting space has moved beyond early adoption, with most organisations already having solutions in place. The statement "overwhelmed by the number of carbon platforms available" captures this well. Organisations are in implementation and optimisation phases rather than initial procurement. Second, the scope three challenge remains persistent. Multiple conversations referenced scope three specifically, with prospects noting it as "time-consuming," collected "manually through a provider," and "not easy" even with systems in place. One prospect confirmed "their scope three is externally verified," indicating the complexity and need for external validation. The NHS contact revealed sector-specific challenges: "unclear methodology" for measuring scope three emissions with "ongoing updates to the NHS portal." Third, organisational restructuring is affecting sustainability priorities. One prospect reported "a company restructure leading to less emphasis on ESG, and any existing targets have been put on hold," whilst another "moved out of ESG and is now focused on industry engagement." Job changes were frequent, with multiple prospects no longer in sustainability roles or having left companies entirely. This suggests the sustainability function is still finding its stable place within organisational structures. Fourth, the in-house versus outsourced dynamic is evolving. Several prospects manage reporting themselves whilst others use consultancies. One specifically "expressed interest in managing it in-house but struggles to get business sign-off," revealing tension between desired autonomy and organisational support. The free tools mentioned by the gallery sector indicate that smaller organisations seek cost-effective solutions. Finally, timing and capacity constraints were ubiquitous. Prospects cited "urgent priorities due to new OGM regulations," being in "SPTI reporting phase," and general bandwidth issues. The sustainability function appears under-resourced relative to expanding requirements.

Recommended Actions

Based on the intelligence gathered, we recommend five specific actions:

1. **Implement a 12-18 month nurture cycle targeting contracted prospects.** With multiple prospects citing contracts expiring in 2027 and beyond, build a systematic follow-up programme aligned to contract end dates. Focus messaging on procurement preparation, evaluation criteria, and emerging requirements that incumbents may not address. Prospects explicitly invited future contact ("follow up next year," "reach out again in July or August"), creating permission-based touchpoints.

2. **Develop scope three-specific positioning and methodology content.** This emerged as the persistent pain point even among organisations with existing solutions. Create resources addressing "time-consuming" manual processes, verification requirements, and methodology clarity. The NHS prospect's "unclear methodology" challenge represents a segment opportunity. Consider offering scope three audits or assessments as a lower-commitment entry point.

3. **Create a budget justification toolkit for sustainability managers.** The prospect who "struggles to get business sign-off" despite wanting to move in-house represents a common scenario. Develop ROI frameworks, time-saving calculators, and risk mitigation narratives that sustainability managers can use internally. Address the "time and budget constraints" objection proactively by quantifying the cost of current approaches.

4. **Build referral pathways for wrong-contact scenarios.** Multiple conversations identified alternative contacts ("Paloma Hemosa," "Sam Davies," "Olivia Fernandez"). Create a systematic approach to warm handoffs where the original contact introduces Furthr, increasing conversion likelihood. Consider offering group-level demonstrations where reporting is centralised, addressing the "managed at the group level in Germany" structural challenge.

5. **Launch competitor displacement campaigns targeting recent implementations.** Prospects using Netscope "just started this process in January" and others who "recently started using their services" are in the critical evaluation period where early challenges emerge. Target organisations 3-6 months into new implementations with content addressing common transition difficulties, integration gaps, and optimisation opportunities. Those who "aren't currently measuring scope 3 emissions" despite having platforms represent cross-sell opportunities.
