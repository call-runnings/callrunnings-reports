Campaign Overview

During April 2026, the campaign achieved 1,518 total dials, resulting in 134 connected conversations. From these connections, 9 meetings were successfully booked with qualified prospects. The disposition breakdown reveals important market insights: 24 prospects were activated and engaged positively with the proposition, whilst 21 indicated 'No, Not Now' suggesting potential future opportunity. 59 prospects provided a definitive 'No, Not Ever' response, indicating market saturation or competitive lock-in. Additionally, 8 prospects referred us to alternative contacts ('No, Not Me - Ref'), and 13 confirmed they were not the right contact without providing referrals. The majority of dials (1,287) resulted in no answer, whilst 19 contacts were attributed to bad data, wrong numbers, or individuals who had left their companies. No email requests or voicemails were logged during this period.

Competitor & Market Intelligence

The competitive landscape is heavily populated, with numerous established players identified during conversations. Watershed emerged as a specific platform in use, with one prospect stating she 'uses a platform called Watershed for carbon reporting and isn't looking to change.' PlanetMark appeared multiple times, with prospects describing recent partnerships: 'they've partnered with PlanetMark for their carbon footprint verification' and noting they 'have only recently started using their services.' Another mentioned 'they're currently using PlanetMark and managing it themselves.' Netscope was identified as a newer solution with one prospect confirming 'SpaceMade is currently using Netscope for tracking their carbon emissions' and that 'they just started this process in January.' The software solution Trace was mentioned by a prospect who stated 'they already use a software called Trace for scope three reporting.' Beyond software platforms, consultancy arrangements proved common, with PwC explicitly mentioned for audit functions: 'their procedures are controlled and audited by PwC.' Generic references to consultants were frequent, with prospects noting they have 'a carbon consultant' or are 'using an external consultant for annual reporting.' The Gallery Climate Coalition and Judy Bicycle Alban Calculators were identified as free tools used by smaller organisations. Sentiment towards existing providers was consistently positive or neutral, with prospects describing solutions as 'straightforward and cost-effective,' indicating high switching costs and satisfaction levels across the market.

Common Objections

The primary objection category centred on existing provider satisfaction and contractual commitments. Multiple prospects indicated multi-year contracts, with specific examples including 'a solution in place that is contractually set for three years' and 'an ongoing contract until April 1, 2027.' This reveals a market with lengthy procurement cycles and high customer retention rates. The second major objection involved recent purchasing decisions, with prospects noting they 'recently entered a procurement phase, indicating they've already made a decision' and 'recently invested in a new reporting tool.' Timing-based objections were frequent, with prospects citing immediate operational pressures: 'currently focused on urgent priorities due to new OGM regulations' and requests to 'reach out in six months' or 'follow up next year.' Budget constraints emerged as a significant barrier, particularly among smaller organisations, with one prospect explicitly stating 'no budget for software' and another describing their tools as 'free and work well for them.' The objection of market saturation was candidly expressed by one prospect who stated they are 'satisfied with their current processes and overwhelmed by the number of carbon platforms available.' Wrong contact objections revealed data quality challenges, with numerous prospects confirming they 'do not handle sustainability reporting' or had moved roles. Several prospects indicated organisational restructuring, with one noting 'a company restructure leading to less emphasis on ESG, and any existing targets have been put on hold.' This suggests broader market uncertainty around ESG prioritisation at certain organisations.

Prospect Questions

Whilst the transcripts captured limited direct prospect questions, the conversational context revealed key areas of concern that prospects care about. Data collection challenges emerged as a central theme, with prospects describing current pain points around manual processes. One mentioned 'they currently face challenges collecting embodied carbon data manually through a provider,' whilst another confirmed that capturing scope three data through existing 'systems' is 'not easy.' The methodology question arose in specialised sectors, with an NHS contact specifically noting 'unclear methodology' around scope 3 emissions and referencing 'ongoing updates to the NHS portal.' Verification and audit requirements surfaced as an implicit concern, with one prospect confirming 'their scope three is externally verified,' suggesting this is a selection criterion. The timing of reporting cycles and budget planning appeared important, with one prospect explaining the challenge of getting 'business sign-off' despite 'interest in managing it in-house.' Another mentioned 'time and budget constraints' linked to their 'financial year.' The level of internal management versus outsourcing was a recurring consideration, with multiple prospects describing how they 'manage their reporting themselves' or handle 'carbon logging' personally. Integration with existing systems and manual processes also emerged, with one prospect specifically mentioning 'currently uses spreadsheets' for scope three reporting, indicating interest in 'more efficient solutions.' These themes collectively suggest prospects are evaluating solutions based on ease of data collection, methodology credibility, verification capabilities, and efficiency gains over manual processes.

Market Themes

Several significant market themes emerged from April's conversations. Firstly, scope 3 emissions reporting represents the primary pain point and area of complexity. Multiple conversations specifically referenced scope 3 as the challenging component, with prospects describing it as 'time-consuming' and noting that 'scope three calculations' require external support. The market shows clear segmentation between organisations at different maturity stages. Some are 'still in the SPTI reporting phase without plans for reductions yet,' whilst others have 'well-established sustainability teams' meeting targets without challenges. This suggests a bifurcated market between early-stage compliance-driven organisations and mature strategic sustainability functions. Organisational structure significantly impacts purchasing decisions. Multiple prospects indicated that carbon reporting is 'managed at the group level' rather than locally, with one specifically stating it occurs 'in Germany and not under his responsibility.' Another mentioned 'each country operates independently, and they have their own tools.' This reveals complex decision-making hierarchies that extend beyond individual contacts. The in-housing versus outsourcing debate is active in the market. Several prospects expressed tension between external consultancy arrangements and internal capability building, with one noting 'interest in managing it in-house but struggles to get business sign-off.' The recent investment cycle is evident, with numerous prospects having 'just started this process in January' or 'only recently started using their services,' suggesting heightened market activity in late 2025/early 2026. Regulatory pressure is driving action, with explicit mentions of 'new OGM regulations' and references to NHS-specific portal updates, indicating sector-specific compliance deadlines are influencing procurement timing. Finally, there are signs of ESG deprioritisation in certain sectors, with at least one organisation experiencing 'less emphasis on ESG' following restructuring. This suggests economic pressures may be causing some organisations to scale back sustainability investments.

Recommended Actions

Based on April's market intelligence, we recommend five specific actions: 1) Develop sector-specific scope 3 methodology collateral, particularly for NHS and regulated industries where 'unclear methodology' is a barrier. Position Furthr as methodology experts who can navigate sector-specific compliance requirements. 2) Create a mid-contract engagement strategy targeting organisations 12-18 months into multi-year agreements with competitors. Several prospects indicated contracts expiring in 2027, suggesting a pipeline opportunity for early relationship building. Frame these as educational conversations rather than sales calls, offering to 'check our website' or share information for 'future reference.' 3) Build an in-housing value proposition that addresses budget sign-off challenges. Several prospects expressed interest in moving from external consultants to internal management but face business case obstacles. Develop ROI calculators and business case templates that sustainability managers can use to secure internal approval. 4) Implement a structured follow-up calendar aligned with financial year cycles and regulatory deadlines. Multiple prospects requested contact 'in a couple of months once their financial year has calmed down,' 'at the end of the year when budgeting starts,' or 'in July or August.' Create trigger-based follow-up sequences around these timeframes. 5) Refine data sourcing to improve contact accuracy, as 21 conversations reached wrong contacts or individuals who had left organisations. Implement a pre-call verification step or invest in higher-quality data sources to improve connection-to-meeting conversion rates. The current 9 meetings from 134 connections (6.7% rate) could significantly improve with better contact targeting, particularly given that 24 prospects were successfully activated, suggesting strong proposition-market fit when reaching the right individuals.
